## Details

### 1. Swipe-to-Match Ratio for Men

**What we need to support:** "900 swipov na 1 coffee date"

**Verified data:**

| Platform | Men's match rate | Equivalent in swipes |
|---|---|---|
| Tinder (median male) | 2.04% | 1 match per ~49 swipes |
| Tinder (average) | 0.6–0.71% | 1 match per 140–167 swipes |
| Tinder (selective swipers <4%) | 11.85% | 1 match per ~8 swipes |
| Hinge (average male) | 2.5% | 1 match per ~40 likes |

Women's match rate on Tinder: **44.39%** — 8.4x higher than men's average.
Source: SwipeStats (analysis of 7,079 real Tinder profiles) [Tier B — independent analytics, methodology published]

**Verdict on "900 swipov" claim:** [NOT SUPPORTED by primary sources]
The most pessimistic real figure is 1 match per 140–167 swipes (Tinder average for men). "900 swipes to 1 date" would require: 1 match per ~140 swipes × 1 date per 6+ matches = ~840 swipes. This is plausible but not directly documented in any single study. The claim is directionally defensible as an extreme case, but "140 swipov na 1 match" is the verified figure. Recommend replacing "900 swipov" with a figure closer to "100 swipov na 1 match" or restructuring to say "desiatky hodín, stovky swipov."

[INTERPRETATION] The 900 figure is likely a folk statistic circulating in dating coach content without a single clean study behind it.

### 2. Time from App Download to First Date

**What we need to support:** "6 mesiacov textovania"

**Verified data:**

- Average American spends **8 months and 11 days** on dating apps before finding a partner. (Shane Co survey, N=1,000+ Americans who met current partner on app) [Single-source — Tier B]
- Men average **~9 months**, women **~8 months** before finding a relationship partner.
- Earlier (2016) survey found ~2 months, but methodology was different and older.
- Millennials and Gen Xers: 9–9.5 months on average.

**Note on what's being measured:** These studies measure time from download to "finding a partner" — not specifically to "first date." The Pew Research 2023 data shows 66% of online daters have been on at least one date, and 50% of mutual conversations begin within 8 hours of matching, but doesn't give a download-to-date timeline.

**Verdict on "6 mesiacov" claim:** [Plausible but not directly verified]
The 8-month average to finding a partner supports that "mesiac za mesiacom" is realistic. "6 mesiacov textovania" as a copy claim is defensible if understood as time actively spent on apps — more conservative than the actual average. Can be used with confidence as directionally accurate. For precise copy, "mesiace" (months, plural, unspecified) is safer than "6."

### 3. Match → Conversation → Date Funnel

**What we need to support:** "200 matchov. 12 rozhovorov. 3 rande."

**Verified data (Tinder, SwipeStats primary analysis):**
- Only **15% of matches become real conversations** [Single-source — SwipeStats]
- **43% of men's Tinder matches result in 0–1 messages** [Single-source — SwipeStats]

**Verified data (Hinge, platform-reported):**
- Hinge claims **90% of matches lead to conversations** — [Single-source, platform-reported, likely inflated]
- Note: Hinge's self-reported number and Tinder independent analysis diverge dramatically. Hinge measures any message exchange; Tinder analysts likely define "real conversation" differently.

**Verified data (individual user tracking, female perspective):**
Lana Li (Hinge "Most Eligible" 2022, tracked data published on Substack lovemelikearobot):
- 719 matches → 408 with at least one message sent (57%)
- 408 conversations → 27 first dates (6.6% conversion from messaging)
- 27 first dates → 1 mini-relationship (3.7%)
- 719 matches → 1 meaningful connection = **0.14% success rate**
[Tier B — single individual's data, female perspective, self-reported]

**Verdict on "200 matchov, 12 rozhovorov, 3 rande" claim:**
The ratios imply: 6% of matches → conversations, 25% of conversations → dates = 1.5% of matches become dates.

Comparing to verified data: match→conversation is closer to 15–57% depending on platform and gender, but conversation→date is closer to 6–7%. The claim's ratios are in plausible range but lean pessimistic — which may be intentional for emotional impact. The numbers should be framed as illustrative examples, not sourced statistics. [INTERPRETATION: This is almost certainly a rhetorical device, not an exact funnel stat.]

**Copywriting note:** The structure (200/12/3) works emotionally. If you want verifiable numbers, "zo 100 matchov — 6 rozhovorov — 1 rande" is closer to what the Lana Li data suggests for women (3.8%). For men, the funnel is likely worse.

### 4. Ghosting Rate

**What we need to support:** Ghosting as a pervasive phenomenon

**Verified data:**
- **60% of US adults** reported being ghosted while dating (Statista, US survey, August 2023, N not specified) [Tier A — Statista citing survey]
- **45%** of US adults admit to having ghosted someone. [Same source]
- **82% of women** report being ghosted on dating apps; **71% of men** [BankMyCell survey, methodology not fully disclosed] [Tier B]
- Gen Zers more likely to have ghosted (77%) than Millennials (61%) [BankMyCell] [Tier B]
- Top reason for ghosting among women: avoiding confrontation (50%). Among men: avoiding confrontation (38%) + profile misrepresentation (>25%). [BankMyCell] [Tier B]

**Academic source:**
- Timmermans et al. (2021, Journal of Social and Personal Relationships): "Ghosting and breadcrumbing: prevalence and association with online dating behavior among young adults" — confirms ghosting is widespread among dating app users. [Tier A — peer reviewed] Full data behind paywall, but abstract confirms high prevalence.

**Verdict:** Ghosting as a copy claim is well-supported. The 60–82% figures are defensible with citation. [Verified across 2+ sources]

### 5. Gender Asymmetry in Messages/Matches

**What we need to support:** Women receive more messages than men — verified.

**Verified data:**

**OKCupid (2009 original OkTrends data, since-deleted blog, widely cited):**
- Women receive **far more messages** than men at every attractiveness tier
- Top 1/3 of women receive 2/3 of all male messages [Single-source — original data deleted, widely cited in secondary sources] [Tier B]
- Women rate 80% of men as below-average in attractiveness; men rate women on a more normal distribution [OkTrends, archived] [Tier B]

**SwipeStats (2024 analysis):**
- Tinder: Women receive average **2,727 messages/year** vs men's **1,224** [Tier B]
- Tinder: Women match rate 44.4% vs men's 5.26% — 8.4x differential
- Top 1% of men receive 16.4% of all female likes; bottom 50% of men share 4.3% [SwipeStats] [Tier B]

**OKCupid messaging asymmetry (more recent):**
- Men like 1 in 3 profiles; women like 1 in 16
- For every 4 first messages a woman sends, a man sends 14
- Women in the 30th attractiveness percentile receive more messages than even the most attractive men [Multiple sources citing OKCupid data] [Tier B — all citing original deleted OkTrends post]

**Verdict:** [Verified] Gender asymmetry on dating apps is one of the most robust findings in online dating research. The directional claim is unambiguous. Multiple independent sources confirm it.

### 6. Benefit of Doubt in Text vs. In-Person

**What we need to support:** It's harder to give benefit of the doubt via text

**Research found:**
- Online profiles and text communication provide **fewer nonverbal cues** than in-person interaction, making targets harder to understand. [Tier B — psychology research summaries]
- The majority of first impressions are formed through **nonverbal signals** (how someone looks, sounds, moves) — text strips these out. [Multiple psychology sources] [Tier A-adjacent — established finding]
- In-person meetings: people tend to **assume good faith** and not expect deception from those physically present. In contrast, text/video makes deception feel more plausible. [Psychology Today, Decision Lab] [Tier B]
- First impressions form within **100 milliseconds** of seeing a face — text delays or eliminates this entirely. [Tier B — widely cited research]
- People **do not expect to be lied to** in person; they naturally give benefit of the doubt in face-to-face contexts. [Tier B]

**Academic link:** No single landmark study specifically on "text vs. in-person benefit of doubt" found. This is an inference from first impressions research + nonverbal communication literature.

**Verdict:** [Single-source class / Inference] The claim is well-supported as an inference from nonverbal communication research, but there is no single study directly comparing "benefit of doubt given via text vs. in person" as a measured variable. The directional claim is safe; citing a specific percentage or study is not.

### 8. Immigrant/Expat Loneliness

**Data found:**
- Global prevalence of loneliness in immigrants: **24.7%** (meta-analysis, 11 studies, N=166,913) — ScienceDirect 2025 [Tier A — peer-reviewed meta-analysis]
- First-generation immigrants who arrived **after age 18** show higher loneliness vulnerability than those who arrived earlier [PMC] [Tier A]
- Factors: language barriers, loss of cultural community, family separation [Multiple Tier A academic sources]
- Refugees show loneliness rates of **15.9–47.7%** (higher than general immigrants) [ScienceDirect] [Tier A]

**Note:** These studies focus on immigrants generally (older adults, refugees, etc.) rather than specifically on young professional expats (25–39 in Vienna). The finding is directionally applicable but the demographic match is imperfect.

**Verdict:** [Verified] 1 in 4 immigrants experiences loneliness — solid academic backing. Applicable to ČS Dating Wien copy as supporting evidence for the social isolation angle. Specify as "immigrants generally" not "Slovak/Czech expats in Vienna specifically."
